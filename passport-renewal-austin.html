Do You Qualify for Mail-In Passport Renewal in Austin?

Most Austin adults can renew their passport by mail using form DS-82, without visiting a passport acceptance facility in person. You qualify for mail-in renewal if all of the following are true:

  • Your passport was issued when you were age 16 or older
  • Your passport was issued less than 15 years ago
  • Your passport is undamaged (water damage, torn pages, or excessive wear may disqualify it)
  • Your passport was issued in your current legal name — or you have legal documentation of a name change
  • You are able to submit the original passport along with your application

If you don't meet all of these criteria, you must apply in person at a passport acceptance facility using form DS-11. Austin Passport Pros determines which process applies to you and makes sure your application is filed correctly.

How to Renew Your Passport in Austin: Step by Step

  1. Complete form DS-82 — the U.S. passport renewal form. Print it, fill it out by hand in black ink, and do not sign it until instructed by acceptance personnel (for in-person applications)
  2. Get a new passport photo — one 2×2 color photo taken within the last 6 months meeting all State Department requirements. Austin Passport Pros provides compliant passport photos
  3. Gather your most recent passport — you must include your actual passport with your renewal application (it will be returned to you with your new one)
  4. Include name change documentation — if your legal name has changed since your last passport, include a certified copy of your marriage certificate or court order
  5. Calculate and include your payment — $110 standard or $170 expedited, payable by check or money order to "U.S. Department of State"
  6. Mail your complete application — to the correct State Department address (standard and expedited applications go to different facilities)
  7. Track your application — online at travel.state.gov using your application information

When Should You Renew Your Passport?

The best time to renew your passport is as soon as it has 6 months or less of validity remaining — even if you have no immediate travel plans. Many countries require your passport to be valid for at least 6 months beyond your travel dates, and enforcement of this rule is strict.

If you have travel coming up, work backwards from your departure date:

  • Travel in 6+ months: Standard renewal ($110) — most economical option
  • Travel in 3–6 weeks: Expedited renewal ($170) — plan immediately
