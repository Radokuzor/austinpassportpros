India e-Visa — The Fastest Option for Austin Travelers

The India e-Visa (also called e-Tourist Visa or ETA) is an electronic travel authorization that most U.S. citizens can obtain entirely online without visiting a consulate. It is the fastest and simplest way for Austin residents to get authorized to visit India.

India e-Visa Types Available

  • e-Tourist Visa: Available for 30-day, 1-year, and 5-year options. Allows double or multiple entry depending on the duration selected. Valid for sightseeing, casual visits, and short-term yoga/wellness programs.
  • e-Business Visa: For attending business meetings, trade fairs, conferences, or establishing business in India. Valid for 1 year with multiple entries.
  • e-Medical Visa: For travelers seeking medical treatment in India. Valid for 60 days with triple entry. Requires a letter from a recognized hospital in India.
  • e-Medical Attendant Visa: For companions of e-Medical Visa holders. Allows travel alongside a patient receiving treatment.

India e-Visa Processing Time from Austin

India e-Visa applications are processed within 72 hours (3 business days) after a complete application is received. Applications can be submitted no earlier than 120 days before travel and must be approved at least 4 days before your departure date. India e-Visa Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for an India e-Visa, your passport must:

  • Be valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned arrival date in India
  • Have at least two blank pages for entry/exit stamps
  • Not be a handwritten or emergency passport

You must also plan to enter India through one of the designated e-Visa ports (major international airports and select seaports). Austin Passport Pros confirms your entry point is eligible before submitting your application.

India Embassy Visa — When You Need a Traditional Visa

Certain travel purposes and visa categories are not covered by the e-Visa system and require an in-person application at the Indian consulate. You will need a traditional India embassy visa if you are:

  • Applying for an Employment (E) Visa to work in India
  • Applying for a Student (S) Visa for long-term enrollment
  • Applying for a Journalist (J) Visa
  • Applying for a Research (R) Visa
  • Traveling on a handwritten or emergency U.S. passport
  • Entering India through a land border crossing not covered by e-Visa
  • A Pakistani national or Pakistani-origin person (not eligible for e-Visa)
