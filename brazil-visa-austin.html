Types of Brazilian Visas Available from Austin

Brazil uses a structured visa classification system. The most relevant categories for Austin travelers include:

VITUR — Brazilian Tourist Visa

The VITUR visa is for tourism, leisure, visiting family or friends, and short-term recreational activities in Brazil. It does not permit the holder to engage in paid work or business activities. Austin residents visiting Brazil for personal travel apply under this category when a visa is required based on current bilateral agreements.

VITEM II — Brazilian Business Visa

The VITEM II is for business travelers conducting activities such as meetings, negotiations, market research, and attending conferences in Brazil. This visa does not authorize paid employment by a Brazilian entity. Austin professionals with business dealings in Brazil — a significant and growing bilateral trade relationship — frequently need this visa category.

VITEM V — Brazilian Work Visa

The VITEM V covers authorized paid employment in Brazil. It requires prior authorization from Brazil's Ministry of Labour and is one of the more document-intensive Brazilian visa categories. Austin professionals relocating to Brazil or taking extended work assignments require this visa.

VITEM IV — Brazilian Student Visa

For Austin residents enrolling in Brazilian educational institutions for extended study programs. Requires an acceptance letter from a recognized Brazilian institution and proof of financial support.

VIAGEM — Transit Visa

Required for some travelers transiting through Brazil en route to a third country. Austin Passport Pros confirms whether your specific itinerary and nationality require transit authorization.

Documents Required for a Brazil Visa from Austin

While requirements vary by visa type, most Brazilian visa applications require:

  • Valid U.S. passport with at least 6 months of validity beyond your travel dates and at least one blank visa page
  • Completed Brazilian visa application form (online and printed)
  • One recent passport-sized photo meeting Brazilian consular specifications
  • Proof of travel arrangements — round-trip flight itinerary and hotel bookings or letter of invitation
  • Proof of sufficient funds — recent bank statements showing you can support yourself during your stay
  • Travel or health insurance documentation (required for some categories)
  • Category-specific supporting documents — business invitation letter (VITEM II), work authorization from Ministry of Labour (VITEM V), school acceptance letter (VITEM IV)
  • Applicable consular fees
